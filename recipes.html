Rainbow Cake
Ingredients:

You'll need 3 x these ingredients for six sponges

  • 125g butter, softened, plus a little extra for greasing
  • 225g plain flour
  • 150g golden caster sugar
  • 3 medium eggs (Very important to use the correct size)
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Edible food coloring (red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple)
For Icing:
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 x 250g tubs cream cheese
  • 350g icing sugar
Method

Heat oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4. Grease 2 x 20cm round sandwich tins and line the bases with baking parchment. Tip all the sponge ingredients, apart from the food coloring, into a mixing bowl, then beat with an electric whisk until smooth.

Working quickly, weigh the mixture into another bowl to work out the total weight, then weigh exactly half the mixture back into the mixing bowl. Pick 2 of your colors and stir a little into each mix. Keep going until you are happy with the color – the color of the batter now will be very similar to the finished cake, so be brave! Scrape the different batters into the tins, trying to spread and smooth as much as possible – but try not to waste a drop of the batter – a rubber spatula will help you. Bake on the same oven shelf for 12 minutes until a skewer poked into the middle comes out clean.

Gently turn the cakes out onto a wire rack to cool. Wash the tins and bowls thoroughly, and start again from step 1 – this time using another 2 colors. Unless you’re making the optional pink layer, repeat one more time to get 6 sponges, all of different colors. Leave them all to cool.

To make the icing, very briefly beat the vanilla and cream cheese with an electric whisk until smooth. Sift in the icing sugar and gently fold in with a spatula. Be careful – the more you work it, the runnier it will get, increasing the chance of splitting.

Smear a little icing on your cake stand or plate – just a splodge to stick the first sponge. Start with the red, then spread with some icing right to the very edge. Repeat, sandwiching on top the orange, yellow, green, blue and finally purple sponges. Spread the remaining icing thickly all over the sides and top of the cake. Blueberry Cheese cake
Ingredients:
  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar 1/4
  • cup melted butter 2 (8 ounce) packages cream cheese, softened 1 cup
  • sour cream 3/4 cup
  • white sugar 1 teaspoon
  • vanilla extract 2 tablespoons
  • all-purpose flour 4 eggs
  • 2 cups frozen
  • blueberries, dry pack 1/3 cup blueberry
  • jelly Add all ingredients to list
Directions

Combine crumbs, 2 tablespoons sugar and butter. Pat mixture into the bottom of a 9 inch Springfield pan.

Mash cream cheese until soft and creamy. Gradually beat in sour cream, 3/4 cup sugar, vanilla and flour. Beat in eggs one at a time.

Pour mixture into crumb-lined pan. Bake in a preheated 325 degree F (165 degrees C) oven for 1 hour or until firm to the touch.

Cool and then remove cake from pan by loosening edges with a knife. Place frozen blueberries on top of cake. Melt jelly and spoon over blueberries to glaze. Chill until ready to serve.

Chocolate Cake
Ingredients:
  • 3/4 cups or margarine, softened
  • 3/4 cup butter or margarine, softened
  • 3 eggs
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 2 cup flour
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 3/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 1/2 cup milk
Icing Method

Beat Butter and Icing sugar (add sugar gradually) until soft and creamy, Add Milk, Vanilla Essence and beat further. Color the icing mixture with a desired color. Once the cake is baked, cut it into two equal size portions. Sandwich it with the icing thereafter

How to make chocolate cake

Pre-heat oven to 350 degrees.

Grease and flour three “6 X1 1/2” round cake pans.

Mix together flour, cocoa powder, baking powder and baking soda. Set aside.

In a bowl, beat butter, egg and vanilla.

Gradually and sugar.Beat on medium to high speed for about 3-4 minutes until well mixed

Alternately combine in flour mixture and milk to batter while beating.

Continue to beat until batter is smooth

Pour equal amount of batter into greased and floured round cake pans

Bake 30 to 35 minutes.

Check with toothpick to see if it is done. Bake a few minutes more, if needed.

Remove from oven and allow cake to cool in pans for a few minutes.

Place cakes on a wire rack, to them allow to completely cool

Ribbon Cake
Ingredients:
  • 1.5 Cups of Butter
  • 4 Eggs
  • 1.5 Cups of Sugar
  • 1.5 Cups of Floor
  • 1/2 Cup Thick Milk
  • 2 leveled teaspoons Baking Powder
Icing Method

Beat Butter and Icing sugar (add sugar gradually) until soft and creamy, Add Milk, Vanilla Essence and beat further. Color the icing mixture with a desired color.

Once the cake is baked, cut it into two equal size portions. Sandwich it with the icing thereafter

METHOD

Beat Butter and Sugar until soft and creamy. Add eggs one by one. Add Vanilla and mix well. Add flour and baking powder gradually in small quantities and mix well. Add milk. Divide the mixture into three or two portions and color with pink, green, and yellow very lightly.

Grease the pan with Butter and cover with flour. Pour the three mixtures alternately in the pan and bake for 20 minutes at 200 degrees centigrade temperature.

Biscuit Pudding
Ingredients:
  • Marie biscuits
  • ¼ milk
  • 4 tablespoon Abraham’s icing sugar
  • 4 tablespoon of chocolate powder
  • 4 tablespoon white butter
Method of Preparation

Put the milk in a small bowl

Spread a tablespoon of milk on the biscuits and place the biscuits each other up to 8 biscuits.

Beat Abraham’s icing sugar, chocolate powder and white butter till smooth and add the remaining tablespoon of milk.

Cover the biscuits using butter and Abraham’s icing sugar mixture using a spoon Top with almonds and freeze for 3 to 4 hours.

Fig & Honey Ice cream
Ingredients:
  • 10 figs finely chopped and soaked in 4 tablespoon of milk for 1 to 2 hours
  • 1kg full cream milk
  • tablespoon Abraham’s corn flour mix in ¼ cup milk
  • 3 table spoon honey
  • ¾ cup Abraham’s rose syrup
  • 200g cream
Method of Preparation

Boil the milk with medium fire for 25 minutes

Add Abraham’s corn flour paste and cook for 5 minutes and remove from fire and wait for 15 minutes.

Add Abraham’s rose syrup and milk and keep it over night

Next day beat cream till fluffy

Cut them into small cubes. Beat till fluffy and do not let it melt

Add cream to it and mix for just few seconds.

Add soaked figs and honey, mix it well and freeze over night

kulfi
Ingredients:
  • 1 kg (5 cups) full cream milk, ¾-1 cup Abraham’s rose syrup
  • 2 table spoon Abraham’s corn flour
  • 75g fresh milk khoya
  • ¼ cup chopped mixed nuts (pista, almonds and cashews)
  • of 2 crushed green cardamom and mint leaves
Method of Preparation

Dissolve Abraham’s corn flour in 1 cup milk and keep aside. Boil the rest of the milk till it is reduced to half in quantity for about 20 minutes in medium fire.

Add Abraham’s rose syrup and Abraham’s corn flour and cook for 4 to 5 minutes more.

Remove from fire and wait for 15 minutes and the add milk khoya, pista, almonds, cashews and crushed leaves.

Fill the mixturein kulfi moulds and Freeze for 6 to 8 hours.

Garnish with Abraham’s rose syrup and crushed mint leaves while serving.
